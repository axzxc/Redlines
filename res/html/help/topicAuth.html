Redlines - Topic (Author)

Return

Closes the currently opened topic view and returns you to the main Redlines hub, where you can browse, open, or manage other topics.

Info

Displays key metadata and diagnostic information about the selected topic, useful for both understanding content scope and verifying data integrity:

  • Number of Primary Sources – Total original materials directly cited in the topic.
  • Number of Secondary Sources – Supplementary or analytical materials supporting the main discussion.
  • Number of Related Topics – Other topics connected via shared themes, authorship, or content relevance.
  • Topic Author – The creator or original compiler of this topic's data.
  • Topic Size – Current disk size of all topic content including sources and metadata.
  • Topic UID – Unique identifier assigned to this topic for internal referencing and node sharing.
  • Topic Hash – A cryptographic fingerprint used to verify the topic’s integrity and detect tampering.

Edit

Enables edit mode for the topic description, allowing you to refine or revise how the subject is introduced or explained. This does not affect your sources or topic structure.

Publish/Published

Marks the topic as ready to be shared across your Redlines network. If auto-publishing is enabled in the settings, updates will trigger re-publication automatically. Large topics may require extra time to fully package and verify before being available to nodes.

Annotations

Displays feedback or commentary from other nodes regarding your topic. These annotations are stored locally and serve as a private review or suggestion log—they are not included when others download your topic.

Primary

Displays a list of all primary sources—documents, media, or datasets that are directly referenced and serve as foundational evidence for the topic.

Secondary

Displays secondary sources that provide contextual support, expert interpretation, or additional data that enhances the understanding of the topic.

Related

Shows connections to other topics in the system, helping users navigate a broader network of linked discussions. Each relation type offers a specific kind of contextual bridge:

  • Other – Miscellaneous relation not fitting the standard categories.
  • Background – Provides foundational context or historical framing to this topic.
  • Follow-up – Continues or builds upon this topic with new developments or events.
  • Contrasting View – Presents an alternative or opposing perspective for balance.
  • Similar Issue – Covers a different subject with notable structural or thematic parallels.
  • Same Author – Created by the same user, offering connected viewpoints or continued thought threads.
  • Expanded Analysis – Delves deeper into specific aspects or implications originally introduced.
  • Case Study – Applies this topic’s concept to a real-world scenario or individual example.
  • Legislation/Policy – Includes laws, regulations, or policy proposals relevant to the topic.
  • Debunked Claim – Addresses and corrects false or misleading assertions within or about the topic.
  • Updates – Provides new information, corrections, or clarifications made after the original topic publication.
