Redlines - Topic (Client)

Return

Closes the currently opened topic view and navigates you back to the main Redlines hub. From there, you can browse through existing topics, open a different one for review, or initiate a new topic session.

Info

Displays key metadata and diagnostics about the selected topic, essential for understanding its scope, structure, and internal consistency:

  • Number of Primary Sources – Total count of original materials (e.g., documents, media, datasets) directly cited as core evidence.
  • Number of Secondary Sources – Count of supplementary materials offering commentary, interpretation, or contextual insight.
  • Number of Related Topics – Number of thematically or structurally connected topics that help broaden understanding.
  • Topic Author – Username or identifier of the individual who originally compiled or authored this topic.
  • Topic Size – Storage size of the topic, including all its assets (sources, metadata, annotations, etc.).
  • Topic UID – A globally unique identifier used to distinguish this topic in the system and enable cross-reference across nodes.
  • Topic Hash – A cryptographic checksum used to ensure the topic’s authenticity and confirm no unauthorized modifications have occurred.

Author Status

Displays the real-time connectivity status of the topic's author, helping you gauge the likelihood of receiving timely annotation responses:

  • Unreachable – The author's client ID (CID) is currently offline or cannot be located; communication is not possible.
  • Connected & Closed – The author is online but has not opened this topic, so they won’t see annotations unless they reopen it.
  • Connected & Open – The author is online and has the topic actively open, making it an ideal time to engage via annotations.

Annotations

Provides access to the private annotation thread sent by you to the author. Here, you can view previously sent annotations and send new ones using the built-in message box. Each annotation has a 30-second cooldown to encourage thoughtful feedback and prevent spamming. All annotations are visible only to you and the author, ensuring privacy.

Primary

Lists all primary sources attached to the topic. These are firsthand materials—such as transcripts, articles, video/audio clips, or datasets—that form the evidentiary backbone of the topic’s claims and arguments.

Secondary

Displays secondary sources used to provide context, expert commentary, or related findings that support the topic’s primary evidence. These materials help enrich interpretation and validate assumptions.

Related

Shows all topics within the system that are linked to this one through shared content, themes, or contributors. Understanding these relations helps users explore a broader narrative or cross-topic insight. Relation types include:

  • Other – General or non-categorized relationships.
  • Background – Provides historical or contextual framing relevant to this topic.
  • Follow-up – Expands upon or updates the topic with new developments or related sequences.
  • Contrasting View – Offers a differing or opposing stance to introduce balance or critique.
  • Similar Issue – Addresses a different subject that shares parallel characteristics or themes.
  • Same Author – Authored by the same individual, which may indicate a continued train of thought or analysis.
  • Expanded Analysis – Takes a deeper dive into a specific facet of the topic introduced at a higher level.
  • Case Study – Applies the concepts of the topic to a practical or specific real-world example.
  • Legislation/Policy – Covers laws, bills, or policy changes directly affecting or derived from the topic's content.
  • Debunked Claim – Identifies and addresses inaccuracies or falsehoods originally tied to the topic.
  • Updates – Reflects new information, corrections, or refinements added post-publication.
